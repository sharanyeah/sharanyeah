context

most people read. i decode. i reverse-engineer why headlines work, what makes copy stick, and the exact moment attention drops. then i rebuild it better. not just products — people, behavior, systems, tone. the invisible mechanics everyone ignores.

right now: obsessed with content that converts without feeling like content. the sweet spot where strategy meets psychology meets actual human language. because the best marketing doesn't scream "buy this" — it whispers "yeah, this gets me."

when i'm not tearing apart landing pages or screenshot-hoarding UX copy, i'm overthinking restaurant menus (why *that* dish description?), analyzing brand voice inconsistencies, and explaining to anyone who'll listen why their CTAs aren't converting.
